Vector Heading Lock

ProNavâ„¢ Angler Vector Navigation will keep your boat on a straight course by correcting for drift caused by wind, waves, or current. This feature is ideal for days where conditions are less than ideal but the bite is good.

  • Maintain a consistent heading and precise speed
  • Follow a specific course over ground (COG) with GPS Vector Heading Lock
  • Achieve a controlled drift while keeping your boat locked on to a constant heading with Compass Vector Heading Lock
  • Keep your drift at the perfect speed by using GPS cruise control or thrust control to select your speed in MPH or based on thrust percentage
  • Adjust the Vector direction at any time using the mobile app, foot pedal, or compatible handheld remote

What is the Difference between GPS Vector and Compass Heading Lock?

GPS Vector keeps you on a constant course over ground. In heavy wind your boat will align with the wind direction while the bow maintains position along the GPS Vector heading line. Compass Heading Lock will keep you going in a constant compass direction but will not keep your boat on a specific course over ground--a crosswind or waves will cause your boat to drift but your trolling motor will maintain the same compass heading of your boat.
